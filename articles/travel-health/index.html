When Health Emergencies Strike Away From Home

"I'm in a foreign hospital" — the doctors don't speak English well, and you can't remember your medication names. "I lost my medication" — your luggage was lost with prescriptions inside. "The resort called an ambulance" — your spouse is panicking, trying to remember your allergies.

It Happens to Travelers Like You

"I had a heart attack in Mexico. I don't speak Spanish, and I couldn't remember if I was on Metoprolol or Lisinopril — I just knew they were for blood pressure. The doctors had to guess at treatment. When I got home, I immediately started keeping my health records on my phone."

— Thomas B., heart attack survivor, age 63
